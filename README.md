## Purpose

The CSI provides a structured analytical method to evaluate how human creative input contributes to the specific expression of an AI-generated work.

The resulting analysis may serve as a structured basis for legal argumentation when assessing the potential copyright protection of a specific AI-generated work.

The framework is intended as an analytical aid and does not replace legal interpretation or case-by-case assessment.

The current version of the index is designed for **AI-generated images**.

The CSI evaluates the user's involvement in the generation process by examining user interactions such as:

- prompting
- iteration and selection
- technical control

Each interaction is assessed according to the **depth of human involvement** and assigned a value between 0 and 1. These values are then **weighted according to their influence on the final generated work**.

The weighted contributions are combined to estimate the **overall share of human creative contribution** in the generated work.

For the full explanation of the method, see the paper.

## Future Extensions

The current article focuses on AI-generated images.

The framework is intended as a starting point for further discussion, application, and development.

Possible future developments include:

- CSI for **AI-generated text**
- CSI for **video**
- CSI for **music**
- CSI for other hybrid creation processes
- automated CSI scoring tools

Readers are welcome to explore, apply, and further develop the framework according to their own needs and interests.

## Methodological Note

The Creation Share Index (CSI) presented in this article is intended as an analytical tool for structured evaluation of human creative contributions in AI-generated works.  

It does not constitute a legally binding standard.
